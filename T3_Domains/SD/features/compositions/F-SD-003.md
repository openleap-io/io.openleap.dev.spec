## SS0. Identity

### 0.1 Purpose
This composition node groups all capabilities for executing billing runs, reviewing generated invoices, and managing credit notes. A billing clerk and finance manager use these features to convert delivered goods and services into posted financial documents that flow to the FI suite.

## SS1. Children & Variability Structure

### 1.1 Group Logic
**Group type:** mixed

**Business rationale:** Billing Run and Invoice Review are mandatory because every deployment needs the ability to generate and approve invoices before they are posted to accounting. Credit Note Management is optional because organizations without a returns or dispute-handling process do not need credit note creation; credit notes may also be handled entirely within the FI suite in simpler configurations.

### 1.2 Sub-Groups

| Sub-group | Children | Group type | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| Core billing | `003-01`, `003-02` | mandatory | Minimum viable billing: generate and review invoices |
| Credit management | `003-03` | optional | Only needed where SD manages the full returns-to-credit cycle |

### 1.3 Rationale for Tree Position
Billing & Invoicing is the terminal step in the SD value chain, consuming goods issue events from F-SD-002 and producing financial documents for the FI suite. It maps to the sd.bil bounded context. It depends on F-SD-001 for order references on every invoice.

## SS3. Quality & Testing Guidance
All leaf features MUST be independently testable. Integration tests MUST verify that a billing run in F-SD-003-01 produces invoices that appear in F-SD-003-02 for review. Credit note tests in F-SD-003-03 MUST verify that `sd.bil.credit-note.posted` is published and consumed by the FI suite.
