## SS0. Identity

### 0.1 Purpose
This composition groups all capabilities for browsing multi-dimensional profitability reports, analyzing contribution margins, and assigning profit centers to cost objects. A management accountant uses these features to evaluate segment-level profitability, drill into revenue and cost drivers, and maintain the profit center hierarchy that underpins internal reporting.

## SS1. Children & Variability Structure

### 1.1 Group Logic
**Group type:** mixed

**Business rationale:** Report Browser and Contribution Margin Analysis are mandatory because profitability analysis without both overview and drill-down is incomplete. Profit Center Assignment is optional because not all deployments use profit center accounting (co.pca) — it is only needed when the organizational dimension requires explicit profit center mapping.

### 1.2 Sub-Groups

| Sub-group | Children | Group type | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| Core analysis | `004-01`, `004-02` | mandatory | Profitability visibility at overview and detail level |
| Organizational assignment | `004-03` | optional | Only needed when profit center accounting is activated |

### 1.3 Rationale for Tree Position
Profitability Analysis is the top-of-the-funnel reporting composition. It consumes results from F-CO-002 (allocated costs), F-CO-003 (standard COGS), and external revenue data from SD. Its output feeds T4 BI dashboards and period-end management reporting (co.rpt).

## SS3. Quality & Testing Guidance
All leaf features MUST be independently testable. Integration tests MUST verify that revenue events from SD cause profitability segment updates within one event cycle. Contribution margins MUST match the sum of revenue minus COGS minus allocated overhead for the same period.
